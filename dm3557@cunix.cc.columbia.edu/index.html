I am currently a Postdoctoral Fellow at the Data Science Institute of Columbia University in the city of New York. I am primarily working with Daniel Hsu and Shipra Agrawal.

Before joining Columbia, I completed my PhD in Computer Science from Harvard University where I was advised by the inimitable David C. Parkes. I received my ME in Computer Science from Indian Institute of Science. In the past, I also interned at Microsoft Research, Redmond.

Research Interests

I am braodly interested in problems at the intersection of economics and computer science. Specifically, I am interested in how AI/ML systems can be integrated in society where where the decisions directly impacts people's lives. In terms of specific fields, I am interested in social choice theory, game theory, online learning, and algorithmic fairness. Presently, I am working on the following research projects.
  • What is the optimal way to design large-scale voting platform that aggregates people's opinions?
  • How do we ensure that an online algorithm, that interacts repeatedly with people, treats different subgroups fairly?
